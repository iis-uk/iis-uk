The International Isotope Society UK Group
26th Annual Symposium

A one day event covering the synthesis and applications of isotopically labelled compounds to be held on Friday, 17th November 2017 at The Møller Centre in Cambridge, UK.

Download Registration Form

A highlight of the meeting will be a talk from Professor Albert Windhorst from the Vrije University Medical Center (VUmc) in Amsterdam on carbon-11 chemistry and why fighting a 20 minute half-life really makes sense.

Other topics covered will include organic synthesis with carbon-14, tritium & short-lived isotopes, new synthetic methodologies relevant to isotopic labelling, lanthanide chemistry, and radiological protection. A detailed programme will follow in due course.

Contributions to a short series of 5 minute flash presentations covering isotope techniques and methods are actively sought — please contact if you would like to give a presentation. As in past years, there will also be an opportunity to present posters at the meeting — please contact if you would like to present a poster.

We are delighted to be returning to The Møller Centre again this year. The venue is approximately one mile from Junction 13 on the M11 motorway and a short taxi ride from Cambridge railway station with its frequent express services to Liverpool Street station and Stansted Airport. The meeting is timed to allow most UK based people to travel to and from the symposium on the same day.
